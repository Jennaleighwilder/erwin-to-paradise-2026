11 · The Career Layer

AI work from the road.

Twenty-one days of content prompts. Networking targets by town. Music you make and own. The "off-grid deployment" flex. Twenty minutes of work a day on this and the trip pays for itself.

21 daily posts · tied to your real stops

Batch these before you leave. Each day you only have to add one photo from that day and hit post. Each post is a hook + an AI architecture insight drawn from what you're physically looking at.

Networking corridor · where to actually meet humans

Each town has one or two real nodes — chambers of commerce, coworking, libraries with Wi-Fi, small-business networks. Schedule 20-minute coffees a week ahead.

Road playlist · prompts for Mureka

One track per day. Drop each prompt into Mureka the night before — by morning you've got a song the kids can sing along to about the exact place they're driving toward.

Field inquiry script · how to network without pitching

"I'm mapping how small tourism and rural businesses handle bookings, websites, customer questions, and seasonal overload. I build practical AI and web systems. I'm not selling anything today — I'm collecting patterns. What's the most annoying digital problem you deal with?"

Use it on: campground owners, cabin rental owners, outfitters, shuttle operators, small museums, chamber directors, ranch-stay hosts, guides, small-town restaurant owners, gift shops with bad websites. Listen. Take notes. Get permission to follow up. Don't pitch. Three months from now one of them will hire you for a real build.

Pre-departure checklist · do these the week before you leave

  • → Write all 21 posts in a draft scheduler (Buffer free tier, or just a Notion doc)
  • → Reach out to 5 Missoula / Bozeman / Rapid City / Billings contacts NOW to schedule a coffee while you're nearby
  • → Set up Buffer / Hypefury / Typefully for queued posting — you won't have signal half the time
  • → Generate first 5 Suno tracks before you leave so you have road tunes from day one
  • → Open a fresh GitHub repo: erwin-to-paradise-2026 — log everything from the trip (photos, GPS, notes) into it nightly. The repo itself becomes a portfolio piece.
  • → Download offline maps + Wikipedia for every state — Wikipedia goes a long way out there
